Many markets around the world performed well, and A-share investors realized the potential of other markets and began to catch up, especially the United States, Japan, and India markets. The domestic ETF market ushered in a wave of pursuit of cross-border ETFs.

  • Core Ideas

    With the Federal Reserve expected to cut interest rates, emerging markets are expected to benefit from marginal improvements in liquidity. India, whose economy is in a period of high growth, is a force that cannot be ignored in emerging markets. Investors may wish to consider participating through stock index ETFs.

  • Reason one

    In 2023, India's Sensex index rose by 18%, ranking first among emerging markets. According to the forecast of the Reserve Bank of India, GDP will increase by 7.2% year-on-year in 2023. Compared with many emerging economies, India is still in the early stage of development. Although its per capita GDP has increased tenfold since 1980, it is still around US$2,500, which is close to China's per capita GDP level between 2006 and 2007. India's penetration of goods and services such as automobiles, electronics, loans and credit cards is still far lower than that of most emerging markets, which may provide potential long-term growth opportunities.

  • Reason two

    In recent years, India has implemented a series of economic reform policies, including liberalizing restrictions on foreign investment in India, unifying India's goods and services tax, passing a new Bankruptcy Law, and increasing the total foreign shareholding limit on the Indian stock market. These measures are important factors in promoting India's economic development and stock market prosperity.

  • Reason three

    The demographic dividend is also an important factor supporting the Indian economy and stock market. While the population structure of major Asian economies is aging, India's population structure is very young and its labor force is abundant, leveraging the potential for economic development. In 2022, the average age of Indians will be 28.7 years old, of which the proportion of people aged 15 to 64 will account for approximately 63.6%.
